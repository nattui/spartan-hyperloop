The Hyperloop Concept

By Elon Musk

The Hyperloop is a new transportation concept: a high-speed train that travels in a near-vacuum tube. The reduced air resistance allows the capsule inside the tube to reach speeds of more than 1000 km/h. Elon Musk, founder of Tesla and SpaceX, suggested the idea in 2013.

The Challenge

SpaceX Hyperloop Pod Competition

In 2015, SpaceX created an international student competition to build Hyperloop prototypes. After setting up a 1.2 km long vacuum tube next to SpaceX headquarters in California, the competition has been taking place every summer with several hundred student teams applying each year.

We Are Spartan Hyperloop

Spartan Hyperloop has been a team on campus since 2015 contributing research to the SpaceX Competition. This year marks the first instance SJSU Spartans will build a full-scale magnetically-levitating pod. Our immediate goal is to win the Levitation Competition by pushing the boundaries of practical technology. Our ultimate goal is contributing to the development of the fifth mode of transportation.
